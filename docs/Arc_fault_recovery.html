Troubleshooting the SunHopper™-D

Arc Fault Diagnosis

Introduction

The arc fault detection system is part of the SunHopper™-D's safety features. This system verifies no arcs are present on the DC lines of the PV system. An arc occurs when a damaged cable or connector makes partial connection which allows the inverter to begin operation, but begins to open a small gap when the inverter is running. This creates a fire hazard where the inverter must be shut down to reduce energy (and thus heat) within the arc until it extinguishes.

Arcs are detected by monitoring the input current waveform and inverter input parameters. Several criteria must be met for the SunHopper™-D to flag a possible arc event.

If an arc is detected, the system will disable the inverter and show an error message on the display and phone app. Up to 5 automatic retries are attempted within 24 hours. The system will remain disabled for 10 minutes and attempt an automatic recovery.

The system requires a manual reset on the device user interface (UI) after 5 faults have been detected within 24 hours.

Diagnosis

An occasional but rare arc fault may occur in normal operation. This requires no intervention and will recover automatically. A persistent fault must be investigated to determine the cause and remedy the system. Check the following after shutting down the PV array:

  1. Verify the PV connections to the SunHopper™-D PV input terminals are tight and stripped to the correct length.
  2. Verify all connections at the PV panels and rapid shutdown equipment (if installed) are tight.
  3. Check the PV cables to ensure the cables are in good shape with no insulation damage.

Some false failures can also occur depending on the installation. Check the following:

  1. If a single conduit is used for another PV array's panels, false failures may be detected as the other inverter's input noise may be detected as an arc. In this case, the SunHopper™-D PV cables would need to be located in a separate conduit
  2. Some older PV inverters may radiate noise from the PV panels themselves. Having a PV panel in close proximity to the SunHopper™-D may also create a false failure. In this case, the other inverter may need additional input filtering, or the PV panels must be moved away from the noisy panels

Disable Arc Fault Detection

The arc fault detector can be disabled if this is not required by local code. Verify local codes before disabling arc fault to ensure your system is installed to applicable building codes.
